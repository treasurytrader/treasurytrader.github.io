<b>Your brain is literally designed to work against you as a trader</b>

MIT researchers studied real traders for weeks. Every trade they took and every emotion they experienced.

Your biggest opp is not the markets, it's your mind and your emotional reactivity.

<b>The Brain
It has 2 operating systems</b>

The amygdala: ancient, fast, and really emotional. It's built for survival so it sees a red P&L, panics, and throws all logic out of the window.

The prefrontal cortex: logical, methodical, slow. This is the one that makes good decisions. Also known as the part I never use.

In the markets, the amygdala is undefeated because survival instincts are triggered by losing money.

<b>What They Found</b>

The bottom third of performers had a 52% emotional correlation between their mood and their results. The top performers? Only 32% correlation.

They were less emotionally reaction, allowing them to be more successful in the markets. Introverts, extroverts, any of the verts can be successful, it's not about personality, the traders who are successful have one thing in common, and that it is the ability to emotionally regulate themselves.

So, become the most boring trader on earth

Green day. Move on.
Red day. Move on.
Big winner. Move on.
Catastrophic loss. Move on.

<b>Signs you're being emotional:
On your last losing trade did you:</b>

- Immediately look for another entry to make it back?
- Size up to recover faster?
- Convince yourself the setup was still valid?
- Feel it in your chest for the rest of the session?

If yes to any of those, your amygdala is 1-0 so fight back.

Build your rules, build you system and follow it! IGNORE THE EMOTION.
